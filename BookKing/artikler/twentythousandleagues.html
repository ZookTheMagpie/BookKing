Twenty thousand Leagues under the Sea

Author: Jules Verne

Published: March 1869

Pages: 340

Summary

Scientist Pierre Aronnax and his colleagues set out on an expedition to find a strange sea monster and are captured by the infamous Captain Nemo and taken aboard the Nautilus submarine as his prisoners.

As they travel the world's oceans, they become embroiled in adventures and events beyond their wildest dreams.

Personal Opinion

A great novel that manages to stay entertaining even when it explains how scientifically things work.

Might have some words no longer in use, though most editions have explanations.

Review by: André Eliassen
